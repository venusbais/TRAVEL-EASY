Gujarat-Jewel Of The West




"Kankaria Lake-A HISTORIC RECREATIONAL SPOT"


Built by Sultan Qutbuddin in the 15th century, this scenic lake was earlier used by the king for bathing and has now become a popular tourist attraction, especially for picnicking with kids.





"Sabarmati Ashram, Ahmedabad-A HISTORIC LANDMARK"


Also known as Gandhi Ashram, this erstwhile residence of Mahatma Gandhi is located on the banks of River Sabarmati in Ahmedabad, about eight hours away from Kutch.





"Vadodara-CULTURAL CAPITAL OF GUJARAT"


Formerly known as Baroda, Vadodara was one of India’s richest princely states and accumulated its wealth from trading diamonds, cotton. Today, it is known for its architecture, classical music and financial superiority.





"Surat-COMMERCIAL AND ECONOMICAL HUB"


Located on the banks of River Tapi, Surat was once a thriving seaport. Fondly called ‘The Silk City’ of India, its sandy beaches, historic landmarks and picturesque tourist spots have something for everyone.





"Bhuj-FASCINATING HISTORIC TOWN"


About an hour and half away from Kutch, the city’s iconic museums, sprawling palaces, local handicrafts and quaint villages are a treat for heritage lovers.





"Rajkot,Dwarka-FAMOUS FOR EVERYTHING GUJARATI"


Centrally located in the Saurashtra area of Gujarat, Rajkot is about four hours away from Dwarka. It’s a modern industrial town and known for its cleanliness index.
