Rajasthan—The Land Of Maharajas




"Jantar Mantar, Jaipur-A POPULAR ASTRONOMICAL OBSERVATORY"


Built in the early 18th century by the erstwhile ruler of Jaipur, Maharaja Jai Singh, the observatory is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site and features amazing astronomical instruments.





"City palace, Udaipur-LARGEST PALACE IN UDAIPUR"


Built by Maharana Udai Singh and his successors, the City Palace in Udaipur is an architectural marvel nestled on the banks of Lake Pichola.





"Hawa Mahal, Jaipur-A FIVE-STOREYED HISTORIC LANDMARK"


Also called as ‘The Palace Of Winds’, the honeycomb shaped palace features 953 jharoka-style windows and was built by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh to allow the court ladies to enjoy the city processions.





"Lake Pichola, Udaipur-CENTRAL NERVE OF THE CITY"


Surrounded by majestic temples and palaces in the heart of the city, the captivating vistas that this enchanting man-made lake offers are a sight to behold.





"Panna Meena Ka Kund- PICTURESQUE HISTORIC STEEPWELL "


Also known as Meena Bawadi, it’s a square shaped stepwell, with adjoining stairs on all four sides and a room on the northern wall. It’s believed this room was used for religious ceremonies before weddings or on popular festival dates.
